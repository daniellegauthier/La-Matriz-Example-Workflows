Reading the Line Graphs

To interpret the momentum plots we are looking at color sequences as wave-like signals of color derived from RGB arrays.

Each RGB array within a sequence is treated as a discrete signal. When converted into complex momentum, it behaves like a wave packet, where:

* Real part → cosine-like component (in-phase wave)
* Imaginary part → sine-like component (quadrature wave)
* Magnitude → wave energy (amplitude)
* Phase → wave direction & interference state

Think of each color as a separate oscillatory mode.

1. Real Part of Momentum → Standing / Traveling Wave

* Represents the in-phase oscillation
* Smooth variation → coherent wave
* Sharp sign changes → reflections or boundaries
* Positive → forward propagation
* Negative → reverse or reflected wave
* Symmetry → standing wave behavior
* Small-amplitude oscillations indicate low-energy but coherent waves.

B. Imaginary Part of Momentum → Phase-Shifted Wave

* Represents the 90° shifted component
* Strong oscillations → rotating or spiral wave motion
* Zero crossings → resonance nodes
* Large swings → phase instability or mode switching

C. Magnitude of Momentum → Wave Energy Womb

* Flat magnitude → energy-conserving wave
* Spikes → localized excitation
* Drops → damping or destructive interference
* Nearly constant magnitude ⇒ stable wave packet. This confirms no energy loss, only phase rotation

D. Phase of Momentum → Wave Direction & Interference

This is the most important wave diagnostic.

Phase Behavior : Meaning
Linear change : Traveling wave
Sudden jumps : Phase / mode change
Oscillatory : Standing or beating wave

* Different RGB arrays → different phase trajectories
* Crossing ±π → wave inversion

E. Trajectory Plot → Wave Path in Space

The trajectory plots show how the wave moves through space over time.

Interpret as:

* Points → wave samples
* Lines → propagation path
* Color gradient → time evolution
* Straight line → uniform propagation
* Zig-zag → interference or reflection
* Closed loops → standing wave or resonance cavity
* Knots / crossings → topological wave modes

Setting the Womb: The Knot Container

0. Land & Lineage Acknowledgement

Begin by orienting to place and inheritance.

This includes:

* A land acknowledgement honoring the ancestral and ecological context of where the work is taking place
* An intentional lineage acknowledgement, informed by the above Color World

This step establishes that the work does not belong solely to the individual, it is held within a wider field of relation.

1. Identify the Block

Name, do not solve, the current block.

The block may appear as:

* Emotional stagnation
* Repetitive relational patterns
* Somatic tension or numbness
* Cognitive loops or loss of meaning

This step locates where the Knot has formed, without forcing interpretation or self-correction.

2. Reading

A short, curated reading to attune the nervous system and symbolic mind.

The reading functions as:

* A tuning fork for shared resonance
* A permission slip to move beyond linear logic
* A softening of internal surveillance

The reading is not instructional; it is atmospheric.

3. Guided Visualization & Meditation

A guided visualization invites participants into the body and imaginal field.

This meditation:

* Establishes internal safety and orientation
* Activates somatic awareness before analysis
* Prepares the psyche to encounter shadow without fragmentation

The aim is not transcendence, but presence.

4. Knot Sequence

The Knot sequence is introduced as the core womb structure.

The Knot:

* Holds contradiction without collapse
* Allows intensity to arise alongside boundaries
* Prevents premature meaning-making

This sequence supports participants in staying with what is entangled, rather than bypassing it.

5. Plot Sequence

Once the Knot is stabilized, the Plot sequence traces how the block came into being.

Here we examine:

* How value, attachment, or survival strategies shaped the story
* Where influence or adaptation altered integrity
* How movement began, stalled, or diverted

The Plot restores narrative continuity without blame.

6. Additional Sequences (As Needed)

Other sequences may be introduced selectively (Pain, Sad, Fem, Direct, etc.), depending on what colors/Gross National Happiness indicators the material calls for.

These sequences are not additive - they are responsive, offering specific pathways for different complimentary oppositions.

7. Physical Trajectory Reflection

Reflect on how the work moved through the body.

This includes noticing:

* Shifts in posture, breath, or tension
* Moments of contraction or expansion
* Where clarity felt embodied rather than conceptual

The body is treated as data, not metaphor.

8. Discussion & Shedding

Participants are invited to share selectively, with emphasis on:

* Accountability rather than confession
* Witnessing rather than fixing
* Naming what is being released, returned

9. Closing

The container should be closed intentionally to protect the findings. This may include:

* A brief grounding statement
* A return to land and lineage
* A reminder that integration continues beyond the session

The womb is sealed, not to trap, but to protect what has been created.

This process is not therapy, diagnosis, or performance. It is a sovereign space where shadow is allowed to exist long enough to reorganize itself, held by color, sequence, body, and consent.
